## 🎯 Real-World Scenarios

### Scenario 1: First Date Prep
```
User: "First date at an art museum. Help!"

AI Coach Response:
"Perfect setting! Art museums provide natural conversation starters.

🎨 **Before You Go**:
- Research 2-3 exhibits you're excited about
- Prepare questions about their favorite artists
- Dress comfortably but stylishly (you'll be walking!)

💬 **Conversation Flow**:
- 'What drew you to this exhibit?'
- 'Do you create art yourself?'
- 'What's your favorite piece so far?'

🎯 **Pro Tip**: Stand next to a thought-provoking piece and ask 
their interpretation. Reveals personality and values!

Remember: They asked YOU out. They already like you. Be yourself!"
```

### Scenario 2: Texting Confusion
```
User: "They haven't replied in 3 hours. Should I double text?"

AI Coach Response:
"Take a deep breath! Three hours isn't unusual at all. 📱

⏰ **Perspective Check**:
- People get busy with work, errands, life
- Response time ≠ interest level
- Anxious attachment can trick us into catastrophizing

✅ **What TO Do**:
- Distract yourself with other activities
- Wait at least 24 hours before following up
- Match their energy when they do respond

❌ **What NOT to Do**:
- Don't send '?', 'hello?', or 'did I do something wrong?'
- Don't post vague social media stories seeking attention
- Don't spiral into worst-case scenarios

Trust me: If they're interested, they'll respond. You've got this!"
```

### Scenario 3: Confidence Crisis
```
User: "I'm not attractive/smart/funny enough for anyone to like me"

AI Coach Response:
"I need you to hear this: Those thoughts are lies your insecurity 
is telling you. 💙

✨ **Truth Bomb**:
- Attraction is subjective - your 'flaws' are someone's favorite things
- Intelligence comes in many forms (emotional, creative, practical)
- Humor isn't about being a comedian; it's about sharing joy

📝 **Exercise**: Write down:
1. One thing you appreciate about your personality
2. One compliment someone once gave you
3. One moment you felt proud of yourself

The right person won't require you to be 'enough.' They'll celebrate 
who you already are. Dating isn't about proving worth—it's about 
finding someone who recognizes it.

Want to tell me what triggered these thoughts today?"
```
